## How It Started

Outbound engagement targeted the CEO and Founder regarding iPlum's potential struggle with 10DLC campaign vetting and deliverability on the incumbent (Bandwidth) stack. In early 2026, iPlum was facing significant friction in scaling their Sole Prop and Low Volume enterprise offerings due to carrier registration delays. I positioned Twilio not just as a carrier, but as a compliance-first orchestration layer. By bringing in our TCR and Porting specialists during the first week of meetings, we proved we could accelerate their campaign approval timeline from weeks to days while also supporting their overall voice and messaging traffic under one communications API.

## Negotiation Tactics

**The Transition Insurance Strategy:** iPlum was hesitant about the upfront cost of moving 37,000 lines. I successfully negotiated the complete waiver of migration fees with our carrier partners. By removing the cost of change, I made it easy for the CEO to justify moving away from Bandwidth’s infrastructure. I also secured tiered pricing for iPlum's voice and messaging rates to incentivize growth, which effectively neutralized the incumbent's pricing structure while delivering greater value in support and compliance.

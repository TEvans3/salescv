## How It Started

AE-led outbound leveraged recent hiring signals and 10K filings to identify Sonos as a high-fit target focused on product expansion within it's entrenched customer base. Discovery revealed a fragmented stack (Salesforce Marketing, Commerce Cloud, Snowflake, Braze) with no unified profile or real-time data routing layer. Early on, I focused on value-seling and building an auditable value model using Sonos' specific conversion and AOV figures rather than industry benchmarks, which helped us build credibility and rapport to discuss our CDP's value with their C-Level executives.

  <Accordion title="E — Economic Buyer">

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Criteria">
    Criteria were shaped during discovery to favor Segment's strengths:
    - **Warehouse-Native**: Must enhance Snowflake.
    - **Real-Time ID Resolution**: Stitching anonymous and known IDs across multiple sources.
    - **Self-Serve**: Marketing must build audiences without Engineering tickets and lift.
    - **Integrations**: 450+ native connectors (MC, Commerce Cloud, Meta, Google Ads).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Process">
    - **RFP-Led Evaluation:** The process was initiated via a formal RFP to vet technical and business requirements. I successfully influenced the criteria to prioritize Segment's strengths, like Deterministic ID Resolution, Schema Enforcement, and AI-assisted Audience Generation.
    - **Value Validation:** Conducted a Value Engineering phase with the Director of Data Insights. This co-investment phase built an auditable financial model that turned a software cost into a 12:1 ROI story.
    - **Technical & Executive Vetting:** The deal moved from CRM/Data team demos to a Final RFP Presentation for the CCO and the CIO.
    - **Strategic Dependency:** The decision was tied to a concurrent Braze implementation, which created a hard technical sequencing deadline we had to meet to avoid project delay.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="P — Paper Process">
    - **Fiscal Year Alignment:** We targeted and hit an October signature to align with the start of Sonos’ fiscal year, ensuring the $656K was prioritized in the new budget cycle.

    - **Security & Compliance:** Navigated a full InfoSec and SOC II compliance review, establishing trust with the VP of IT.
  </Accordion>

## Negotiation Tactics

**Value Anchoring:** By validating the ROI benefit with the CIO first, the \$656K price point was viewed as a 12:1 ROI rather than a cost.
**ARR Protection:** Bundled Business Critical Services (BCS) and Premium Support into the total contract, allowing procurement to see one package while keeping the underlying software ACV healthy.

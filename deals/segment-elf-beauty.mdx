## How It Started

After losing a previous CDP compete in 2021 to a lower-cost incumbent, I re-engaged the account in late 2024 by identifying a critical gap in their ability to execute real-time, predictive use cases. The "Beauty Squad" loyalty program had grown to 5M+ members, but their existing stack was limited to batch-based processing, leading to stale personalization and high manual overhead. I leveraged hiring signals within the Data Science team to find my Champions and began a "bottom-up" build of a business case focused on Warehouse-Interoperability and out-of-the-box Predictive AI.

## MEDDPICC

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="M — Metrics">
    The business case was validated by a BVA for 5.3M Beauty Squad members:
    - **Retention Revenue**: 1% churn reduction = **\$3.4M annual impact**.
    - **Incremental Revenue**: 5% of members buying one additional "Best Seller" = **\$1.8M impact**.
    - **Efficiency**: 5% CAC reduction through optimized targeting = **\$3.7M savings**.
    - **ROI**: Total projected net benefit of **\$41.2M over 3 years**.
    - **Speed to Value**: Payback period of **less than 6 months** post-implementation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="E — Economic Buyer">
    **Chief Digital Officer**. Focused on ensuring the CDP wasn't just a "database" but a revenue-generating personalization engine. We moved the needle by focusing on the "Cost of Delay"—demonstrating that every 3 months without Segment cost the brand **\$3.6M** in unrealized benefits.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Criteria">
    - **Predictive AI Maturity**: Out-of-the-box LTV and Likelihood to Purchase traits.
    - **Warehouse Interoperability**: Real-time sync to **Snowflake** via Linked Audiences.
    - **Activation Velocity**: Sub-second trait updates for real-time web/app personalization.
    - **Identity Resolution**: 360-degree view across web, app, and POS purchases.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Process">
    A high-velocity, competitive cycle compressed into Q1/Q2 2025:
    - **Discovery**: Identified failures in the incumbent’s AI model refresh rates.
    - **Technical Prep**: Deep-dive sessions with Data Science on "Composable vs. Segment."
    - **Final Demo Day**: Virtual presentation to 10+ stakeholders focusing on Predictive AI.
    - **Executive Readout**: Final ROI validation with the CDO and VP of Data.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="P — Paper Process">
    - **Pricing Transparency**: Modeled API call volume (1B/year) to provide cost certainty.
    - **Validation**: Leveraged professional services to de-risk the 3-month implementation timeline.
    - **Security**: Cleared review regarding PII data residency and Snowflake sync protocols.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I — Identify Pain">
    - **Data Refresh Lag**: Existing models took 24+ hours to refresh, making marketing always reactive.
    - **Technical Debt**: Heavy reliance on manual engineering to "stitch" data together for activation.
    - **Fragmented Identity**: No clear way to link high-value purchase signals from OrderGroove to real-time web behavior.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="C — Champion">
    **VP of Data and Innovation**. She became the key advocate once we proved Segment could work *with* their warehouse strategy rather than against it. She personally led the internal charge to evaluate Segment against "Composable-only" vendors.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="C — Competition">
    - **Incumbent (Displacement)**: Defeated by proving Segment's superior AI "out-of-the-box" capability and real-time latency.
    - **Composable (Hightouch)**: Defeated by proving that a "Warehouse-only" approach would take quarters to achieve value, whereas Segment offers "Speed to Value."
    - **mParticle**: Dismissed due to lack of deep warehouse interoperability and AI maturity.

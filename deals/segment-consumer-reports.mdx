## How It Started

Inbound signal via Segment's self-serve motion — Cody Juric, Sr. Growth Product Manager, had used Segment at three prior companies (Cover Hound, Propeller Health, Section 4) and had been brought into CR's core product team after their Upkept app was sunset. He was now Product Lead for Data & Personalization and had identified a clear gap in CR's stack: no real-time data router, no unified customer profile, and a 24–72 hour delay getting data into downstream tools. He reached out to Segment because he believed it was the solution — I had to work with him to build the internal business case and navigate the org to get it bought.

## MEDDPICC

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="M — Metrics">
    The business case was built around CR's 5M+ member base and four core KPIs:
    - **LTV**: Higher conversion and lower churn via real-time personalization.
    - **MAUs**: Increasing visit frequency and engagement depth.
    - **Retention**: 1% improvement on 5M members = **$2M in retained revenue**.
    - **Operational ROI**: Quantified the reduction in engineering hours spent manually reconciling IDs across Adobe, Monetate, and Acxiom.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="E — Economic Buyer">
    **Suman Veeramalla, CDO**. Initially hesitant to sign a 2-year deal without a permanent CTO in place or the Deloitte assessment complete. Unlocked by offering a 1-year term at the same $523K price point, eliminating the term objection entirely while protecting ARR.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Criteria">
    1. Real-time event collection (Web, iOS, Android, Server-side).
    2. Schema enforcement (Protocols) — violations blocked.
    3. ID Graph setup: stitching anonymous IDs, member IDs, and print IDs.
    4. Reverse ETL from Databricks for profile traits.
    5. Profile API for < 5ms site personalization.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Process">
    18-month evaluation: Discovery (Late 2024) → Multi-threaded expansion (Q1 2025) → Full-day Onsite Workshop in Yonkers (5/22) → 3-Month Technical POC → POC Readout and Legal/Contracting (Oct 2025).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="P — Paper Process">
    Negotiated a custom liability cap: the greater of $2M or 5x annual contract value. Resolved a 2-week legal fight regarding indemnification carve-outs for information security obligations to clear CR's stringent nonprofit compliance requirements.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I — Identify Pain">
    - **72-hour Data Latency**: Personalization was running on stale data.
    - **No Unified Profile**: Multiple identity systems (Adobe, Monetate, CDH) were not stitched together.
    - **Monetate Overutilization**: Tool being used for logic it wasn't built for, causing front-end caching limits.
    - **Build vs. Buy**: Data Office believed they could replicate Unify; countered by proving the engineering cost to build < 5ms Profile API latency internally.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="C — Champion">
    **Cody Juric**. Textbook champion who built the internal data maturity model and PRDs without me in the room. He managed internal stakeholder alignment even during his paternity leave and provided the "playbook" on how to navigate the Head of Engineering.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="C — Competition">
    **vs. Build In-House**: Decisive win by proving speed-to-value and reducing DWH join complexity.

## Technical Validation

Personally led a full-day onsite workshop in Yonkers (5/22/25) for 5+ stakeholders. The following POC validated:
- **ID Resolution**: Stitched anonymous and member IDs across all CR systems.
- **Reverse ETL**: Pulled `donor_tier` from Databricks into Segment Profiles.
- **Real-time Personalization**: Profile API used to serve dynamic homepage themes based on computed traits.
- **Mobile Activation**: Triggered personalized push notifications via Braze.

## Negotiation Tactics

**Trade Term for Protection:** When Suman balked at a 2-year commitment, I offered a 1-year term at the 2-year price point ($523K). This removed the "permanent CTO" blocker while securing the full ARR ask.

**Price Anchoring:** Modeled the cost of API calls ($0.000167/unit) vs. the MTU equivalent ($0.0089/unit) to prove a 53x unit-cost advantage, successfully closing the pricing objection.

---

## Competitive Differentiation

**The Databricks Play:** Instead of competing with the Data Office’s warehouse strategy, I brought in the Databricks partner team to show how Segment would *drive* more workloads to their warehouse. This transformed a potential "build-in-house" detractor into a strategic advocate.

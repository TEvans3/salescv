## How It Started

Inbound signal via Segment's self-serve motion — Cody Juric, Sr. Growth Product Manager, had used Segment at three prior companies (Cover Hound, Propeller Health, Section 4) and had been brought into CR's core product team after their Upkept app was sunset. He was now Product Lead for Data & Personalization and had identified a clear gap in CR's stack: no real-time data router, no unified customer profile, and a 24–72 hour delay getting data into downstream tools. He reached out to Segment because he believed it was the solution — I had to work with him to build the internal business case and navigate the org to get it bought.

## MEDDPICC

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="M — Metrics">
    The business case was built around CR's 5M+ member base and four core KPIs:
    - **LTV**: Higher conversion and lower churn via real-time personalization.
    - **MAUs**: Increasing visit frequency and engagement depth.
    - **Retention**: 1% improvement on 5M members = **$2M in retained revenue**.
    - **Operational ROI**: Quantified the reduction in engineering hours spent manually reconciling IDs across Adobe, Monetate, and Acxiom.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="E — Economic Buyer">
    **Suman Veeramalla, CDO**. Initially hesitant to sign a 2-year deal without a permanent CTO in place or the Deloitte assessment complete. Unlocked by offering a 1-year term at the same $400K price point, eliminating the long-term commitment objection while protecting ARR.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="D — Decision Criteria">
    1. Real-time event collection (Web, iOS, Android, Server-side).
    2. Schema enforcement (Protocols) — violations blocked.
    3. ID Graph setup: stitching anonymous IDs, member IDs, and print IDs.
    4. Reverse ETL from Databricks for profile traits.
    5. Profile API for < 5ms site personalization.
  </Accordion>
